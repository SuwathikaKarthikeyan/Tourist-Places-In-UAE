Burj Khalifa

The Tallest Building in the World

About the Burj Khalifa

The Burj Khalifa is the tallest building in the world, located in Dubai, UAE. Standing at a height of 828 meters (2,717 feet) with 163 floors, it was completed in 2010. Designed by architect Adrian Smith and built by Emaar Properties, it is a symbol of Dubai's rapid growth and modernity. The Burj Khalifa offers breathtaking views from its observation decks and is a key feature in the city's skyline. It holds numerous records, including the highest observation deck and the longest flight of stairs in the world.

Interesting Facts

  • It took over 6 years to build.

  • Over 12,000 workers were involved in its construction.

  • The Burj Khalifa has the highest observation deck in the world on the 148th floor.

  • The building is made up of 103,000 square meters of glass.

History

The Burj Khalifa was designed by architect Adrian Smith and built by Emaar Properties. It was constructed as part of a larger project to transform Dubai into a major global city. Its construction involved thousands of workers and took several years to complete.
